🔑 The Final Piece Now comes Transformers — the architecture invented in 2017 that powers ChatGPT, Claude, Google Translate, and nearly every modern AI you've heard of.

Before Transformers, AI processed words one at a time in order, making it slow and forgetful on long sentences. Transformers changed everything with one powerful idea: let every word look at every other word at the same time. This is called attention.

👀
PART 2
The Attention Mechanism
▼
The Core Idea Read this sentence: "The cat sat on the mat because it was tired."
What does "it" refer to? Your brain instantly knows — the cat! You paid more attention to "cat" than to "mat" when processing "it." That's exactly what the attention mechanism does.

🏫 The Classroom Analogy

Think of a classroom where every student (word) can pass notes to every other student:

  1. Each student writes a Question: "What am I related to?" → called the Query
  2. Each student wears a Name Tag describing what they are → called the Key
  3. Each student holds Information about their meaning → called the Value

When "it" wants to figure out what it means, it sends its Question to every word, compares against each word's Key for a match score, then absorbs the highest-scoring word's Value. "cat" wins → "it" means the cat!

💡 Key Insight You don't need to memorize Query / Key / Value. The important thing: attention lets every word look at every other word simultaneously to figure out context. That's what makes Transformers so powerful.

Before Transformers, models called RNNs processed words one at a time, left to right — by the time they reached "it" in a long sentence, they might have forgotten "the cat." Attention solves this completely.

PART 3
The Transformer Architecture
▼

A Transformer has a clear structure. Here's the simplified pipeline:

  1. Tokenize & Embed
    Break text into tokens, convert each to a number vector (embedding). You already learned this in Week 25!
  2. Add Position Info
    Since the Transformer sees all words at once, we add positional encoding so the model knows word order. Think of numbering seats in a classroom.
  3. Self-Attention
    Every word calculates attention scores with every other word. Transformers use multi-head attention — running attention multiple times in parallel, each looking for different relationship types (grammatical, semantic, positional…). Like multiple study groups each focused on a different aspect!
  4. Feed-Forward Neural Network
    After attention, each word's updated representation passes through a regular neural network (like your Week 23 work) for additional processing.
  5. Repeat!
    Steps 3–4 stack many times. GPT-2 has 12 layers. GPT-3 has 96 layers. GPT-4 ~120 layers. More layers = more capacity to learn complex patterns.
🔮
PART 4
How ChatGPT Generates Text
▼
🤯 The Surprising Secret ChatGPT, Claude, and all modern LLMs do one incredibly simple thing over and over: Predict the next word. That's it. When ChatGPT writes a paragraph, it's not planning ahead — it predicts one token, adds it, and repeats.

Here's the process:

  1. You type: "The cat sat on the"
  2. The model assigns probabilities to every possible next word
  3. "mat" wins (highest probability) → it picks "mat"
  4. Input becomes "The cat sat on the mat" — predict again!
  5. Repeat hundreds of times to generate a full response

Why does it sometimes make mistakes? The model picks one word at a time using probabilities. There's built-in randomness called "temperature" to keep responses creative. And since the model always picks the most "plausible sounding" next word, it can sometimes hallucinate — say something confidently that isn't true.

📊 Fun Fact When Claude writes you a 500-word response, it has made roughly 500+ individual next-token predictions, one after another, each using your entire conversation as context.

PART 5
Subword Tokenization
▼

In Week 25, you learned tokenization (splitting text into words). LLMs use a more advanced version: subword tokenization — uncommon or long words get split into smaller pieces.

📚 Vocabulary Size A typical LLM uses ~50,000–100,000 tokens. By combining smaller pieces, it can represent any word in any language without needing a separate entry for each one.
🍓 Why "entrepreneur" Tricks LLMs The model sees ["entre", "pre", "neur"] — never the individual letters! That's why counting letters or spelling backwards is hard for LLMs.
🔢 Context Windows When GPT-4 has a "128K context window," that means 128,000 tokens — roughly 96,000 words.
🌳
PART 6
The Transformer Family Tree
▼

Not all Transformer models are the same! There are three main types:

Encoder-Only

Understanding Text

Example: BERT (Google)
Used for sentiment analysis, search ranking, text classification.
📖 Like a great reader who analyzes text deeply, but can't write new content from scratch.
Encoder-Decoder

Transforming Text

Example: T5 (Google)
Used for translation (English → French), summarization, Q&A.
🌍 Like a translator who reads in one language and writes in another.
Decoder-Only

Generating Text

Examples: GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama
Used for chatbots, code generation, creative writing, reasoning.
✍️ Like a great writer who can continue any story or answer any question.
🔑 Key Insight ChatGPT and Claude are decoder-only Transformers. Being really good at predicting the next word requires deep understanding of language, logic, and even reasoning!

PART 8
Using Transformers in Python
▼

The Hugging Face library makes it incredibly easy to use powerful pretrained models. No training required!

# Sentiment Analysis in 3 lines!
from transformers import pipeline
classifier = pipeline("sentiment-analysis")
result = classifier("I absolutely loved this movie!")

Compare to Week 25: Last week you had to tokenize text, extract TF-IDF features, train a model, then predict. With a pretrained Transformer, all of that is done for you — and the accuracy is usually much higher!

The pipeline also supports many other tasks:

text-generation
GPT-2 text writing
summarization
Shrink long text
ner

question-answering
Answer from context

REVIEW
Key Takeaways
▼
01
Transformers are the architecture behind ChatGPT, Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, and nearly every modern AI. Invented in 2017.
02
Self-attention lets every word look at every other word simultaneously to understand context.
03
ChatGPT generates text by predicting the next token one at a time, adding it, and repeating. Called autoregressive generation.
04
LLMs use subword tokenization — words get split into chunks. That's why counting letters and math can be tricky for them.
05
Three types: Encoder-only (BERT, for understanding), Encoder-Decoder (T5, for translating), Decoder-only (GPT-4, Claude, for generating).
06
Modern LLMs have billions to trillions of parameters. Your Week 23 NN had ~100,000 — GPT-4 has roughly 18 million times more.
07
With Hugging Face, you can use a state-of-the-art Transformer in 3 lines of Python — no training required. This is transfer learning!
08
Everything connects — tokenization, embeddings, neural networks, loss functions from your previous weeks all come together in Transformers.
